LABOUR PARTY

The Labour Party was formed in 2002 as the Party for Social Democracy by the Nigerian Labour Congress. The party advocates for various issues as a member of the opposition; from the increment of minimum wage to fair rights.

ALL PROGRESSIVE CONGRESS

This political party was formed in February 2013, as a result of a merger of Nigeria's three largest opposition parties. The current president of the Federal Republic of Nigeria is from this party.

ALL PROGRESSIVES GRAND ALLIANCE

At the legislative eleections held on 12 April 2003, the party won 1.4% of popular votes and two of 360 seats in the House of Representatives but no seats in the Senate. The party won 3.3% of the presidential votes in 2003.

SOCIAL DEMOCRATIC PARTY

The Social Democratic Party of Nigeria is a centre-left political party. It was created by former military head of state Ibrahim Babangida via a democracy project. The party calls for concerted efforts to improve welfare and fight for social justice.
