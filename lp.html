LABOUR PARTY

The Labour Party is a social democratic political party. It was formed in 2002, as the Party for Social Democracy by the Nigerian Labour Congress. The party advocates for various issues as a member of the opposition, from the increment of minimum wage to other issues. In the 21 April 2007 Nigerian National Assembly election, the party won 1 out of 360 seats in the House of Representatives and no seats in the Senate. The Party's flag bearer in Ondo State, Olusegun Mimiko, emerged the state governor after winning a judicial challenge. Some of the party's former notable members are Dr. Olusegun Mimiko, the former Governor of Ondo State and the only Labour Party Governor in Nigeria. Former Governor of Oyo State Chief Alao Akala, Adams Oshiomole, the former Governor of Edo State and suspended Chairman of the All Progressives Congress, The LP Candidate for Delta State, Ogboru.
